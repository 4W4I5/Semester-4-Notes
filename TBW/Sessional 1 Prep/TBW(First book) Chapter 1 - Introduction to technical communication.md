## 1.4 The importance of Teamwork
- ###### Collaboration
	- Collaboration is highly sought out by employers as it helps employees 
		- Brainstorm new ideas and conseus
		- Make decisions
		- Solve problems
		- Determine Team roles
		- Assign tasks
		- Complete work by team-determined deadlines
- ###### The Problems with 'Silo Building'
	- It is a metaphor, talks about people who believe that they have no responsibilities if it is outside their department. Acting as if no other department's concerns are valuable
	- To be effective, companies need an open line of communication within and between departments
- ###### Why Teamwork is important
	- Diversity of Opinion
		- Need the opinion of people from different work background to look at a problem from every angle
	- Checks & Balances
		- One persons' good idea might affect someone else negatively therefore there is a feedback system that checks and balances other people
	- Broad-Based Understanding
		- Always better to get everyone involved so that no one is surprised by the decided outcome
		- Talk it out with everyone 
		- Done through Team projects, which provide multiple points of view are shared and improved communication allows people to see the bigger picture
	- Empowerment
		- Whenever a person provides their input they inherently have a stake in the project as well thus boosting morale and productivity
	- Team Building
		- When everyone in the company has the same goal of corporate success, customer satisfaction and quality production. It provides a greater sense of a team, a collaborative workforce
		- We are in this together working toward a common goal

	- Teams consisting of people from different areas of expertise and different ages, genders, cultures and races
- ###### Dispersed Teams
	- Teams consisting of people in different times and spaces. Cannot always be face to face with them, have to resort to online ventures to collaborate together

## 1.6 Challenges to Effective Teamwork
- ###### Human Performance Improvement
	- Focuses on possible causes for collaborative breakdowns such as:
		- Knowledge
			- Some people do not know how to perform a task 
		- Resources
			- Something might not be available at that very moment when it is needed
		- Processes
			- Everyone must know their responsibilities, to maintain effective collaboration the team should set and maintain effective procedures
		- Information
			- A team must be supplied with up to date information
			- It also must be devoid of any jargon if it is sent to any team member who can not interpret it
		- Support
			- Teams need money for staffing, personnel or equipment
			- Support is also managerial as well. Managers boost morale
		- Wellness
			- A person's health and wellbeing is also an important factor, a stressed individual is more susceptible to arguments, missed deadlines, erratic work schedules and poor quality
		- Acknowledgement
			- Upon success management needs to reward staff with promotions, merit raises, bonuses and awards
		- Trust
			- Team members who trust each other are open in their brainstorming, compliment each others' efforts and work effectively towards a common goal
		- Leadership
			- A team needs a direction, successful teams work well when their leaders provide clear deadlines, responsibilities, task goals and fair-minded leadership 
		- Efficient Communication
		- Too much emailing back and forth is overwhelming, only send what needs to be sent and keep it short
